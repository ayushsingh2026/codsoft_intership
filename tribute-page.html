INDIAN ARMY

INTRODUCTION

  • Founded 26 January 1950, 73 years ago (as current service).
  • The primary mission of the Indian Army is to ensure national security and national unity, to defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and to maintain peace and security within its borders.
  • Until the independence of India, the "Indian Army" was a British-commanded force defined as "the force recruited locally and permanently based in India, together with its expatriate British officers". the "British Army in India" referred to British Army units posted to India for a tour of duty.
  • In 1776, a Military Department was created within the government of the East India Company at Calcutta.

FIELD FORMATION

  • COMMAND:

    Indian Army has six operational commands and one training command.

  • CORPS

    A command generally consists of two or more corps. Indian Army has 14 Corps each one commanded by a general officer commanding (GOC), known as the corps commander, who holds the rank of Lieutenant General.

  • DIVISION:

    Each division is headed by GOC (division commander) in the rank of major general. It usually consists of three to four Brigades

  • BRIGADE:

    A brigade generally consists of around 3,000 combat troops with supporting elements. An Infantry Brigade usually has three Infantry battalions along with various Support Elements.

  • BATTALION:

    Composed of four rifle companies.Commanded by a battalion commander who is a Coloneland is the Infantry's main fighting unit.

  • COMPANY:

    Composed of three platoons.Commanded by a company commander who is a major or lieutenant-colonel.

  • PLATOON:

    Composed of three sections.Commanded by a platoon commander who is a JCO.

  • SECTION:

    Smallest military outfit, with a strength of 10 personnel. Commanded by a section commander of the rank of Havaldar.
